Potatoes

  • Regular Potatoes: skins are the most nutritious part (antioxidants), but potatoes generally are high in pesticide content & skins are most exposed; solution - buy organic potatoes & eat skin on.
    • Purchase - buy organic to reduce pesticide exposure. Of modern varieties, Russet is highest in nutritional content but also has the highest glycemic index (although colorful potatoes are far superior).
    • Storage - store "new" potatoes (a type of potato w/ lower GI than regular "old" potatoes) in fridge b/c of rapid spoiling. Keep old potatoes in cool, dark area.
    • Preparation - to reduce GI of potatoes: cook, then chill overnight, before reheating & eating; this modifies the starch into a less easily digestible form. Additionally, cooking potatoes in fat slows digestion. Consume with skin on.
      •   
  • Sweet Potatoes: much lower GI than normal potatoes (45 vs 75-100), richer in antioxidants.
    • Purchase - choose varieties with the darkest skin/flesh (e.g. yams, which are a variety of sweet potato).
    • Preparation - cook by roasting or baking, which doubles antioxidant value (don't boil). Consume the skin with the flesh.
