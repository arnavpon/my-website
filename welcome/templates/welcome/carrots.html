Carrots

  • Purchase - avoid baby carrots (the outer skin, which has been disposed of, is much more nutritious than the fleshy interior). The freshest carrots are those with the green top still attached.
  • Storage - store carrots after cutting the top off to preserve moisture.
  • Preparation - carrots are more nutritious cooked b/c heat increases beta-carotene BA, however boiling causes leeching so steaming or sautéing are preferable. Also, chop the carrots after  cooking to maintain highest nutrient content. Eating carrots with fat is best since vitamin A is fat soluble. If you can find them, buy purple carrots which are high in anthocyanins.
