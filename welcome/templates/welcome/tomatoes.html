Tomatoes

Active compound is lycopene, which protects from UV radiation.

  • Purchase - when purchasing any kind of tomato, look for those with the darkest red color = highest in lycopene. Tomatoes are low GI fruits, and the smaller the tomatoes the greater the sweetness & nutrition. Grape tomatoes are high in nutrition, but when purchasing make sure none of the tomatoes are shriveled (they lose water easily). Currant tomatoes are most nutritious, followed by grape, then cherry. Canned tomatoes & pasta sauces are highly flavorful & nutritious (heat used in canning increases lycopene) but avoid containers with BPA
  • Storage - do not store tomatoes in the fridge (reduces flavor & aroma). Keep at room temperature, stem side up, and eat within a few days.
  • Preparation - eat tomatoes whole (skin, seeds, and flesh); cooking increases BA of lycopene (the longer the better).
