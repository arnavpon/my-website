Cruciferous Vegetables

Family includes arugula, broccoli, Brussels sprouts, cabbage, cauliflower, kale, radish, turnips

  • Modern varieties are same as wild ancestors in terms of nutritional content. Glucosinolates are active compound (highest in kale & Brussels sprouts), but also cause bitterness.

  • Broccoli:
    • Purchase - to purchase fresh, buy whole heads & look for dark green crowns, firm green/smooth stems.
    • Storage - chill immediately after bringing home, eat within a day or two.
    • Preparation - steaming & sautéing in garlic/olive oil are the most effective ways to cook broccoli.
      •   
  • Brussels Sprouts:
    • Purchase - to buy fresh, look for bright green sprouts with tightly wrapped leaves.
    • Storage - chill immediately after coming home.
    • Preparation - steam, roast, or sauté but remember they become more bitter & soggier the longer they cook.
      •   
  • Cabbage: red > green.
    • Purchase - look for compact heads that are firm & heavy.
    • Storage - keeps for a long time in the fridge.

  • Cauliflower: superior to cabbage. Purple > green > white.
    • Purchase - look for heads without spots or bruises.
    • Storage - keeps for long time in fridge.
    • Preparation - steam or sauté.
      •   
  • Kale: the best of the crucifers. Purple > green leaves.
    • Preparation - most nutritious when eaten raw as salad or sauteed in olive oil or baked at 325 degrees into chips (coated with olive oil & salt).
